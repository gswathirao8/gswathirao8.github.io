Bio

I am a 4th-year undergraduate at Indian Institute of Technology Madras majoring in Chemical Engineering.

My research interests lie in computational and process systems engineering; with emphasis on data-driven techniques to model the behavior of dynamic chemical systems. Such domain-informed models are a combination of physically insightful white box approaches and computationally powerful black box approaches, presenting new possibilities for automation and discovery of chemical processes.

I love to draw and paint in my free time. Usually, I experiment with different artforms an painting mediums. I also enjoy listening to music and reading novels. If you want to talk about paintings, books, music and ofcourse, discuss about science and research, I'm always up for a chat!

Personal email: swathigrao8 [AT] gmail.com

Overview

My previous explorations have been interdisciplinary projects marrying Data-Science and Chemical Engineering to identify, predict and control the behavior of various chemical processes.

Previously, I have worked with Prof. Himanshu Goyal to capture mesoscale structures in multiphase CFD simulations using a density-based clustering algorithm. I spent summer 2021 at CAPS Lab, where I worked with Prof. Hariprasad Kodamana to build NLP based recommendation systems for optimal process conditions for H2 production. This summer, I interned with Prof. Vinay Prasad at University of Alberta, where I developed Attention based architectures for prediction in adsorption columns and CSTRs. Currently, I am working with Prof. Nirav Bhatt on my B.Tech thesis to develop “Chemical Computers”, a set of programmable CRNs for algebraic computations.
